Stawamus Overlook

Design Intent

A home that exists in conversation with the Chief. The entire design orbits around a single uninterrupted sightline from the entry threshold through the central living volume to the granite face of the Stawamus Chief. Every material, every proportion, every threshold was calibrated to frame and amplify that relationship.

The plan is organized as two offset bars connected by a glass bridge — the public volume (living, kitchen, dining) faces the Chief, while the private wing (bedrooms, studio) is rotated 12 degrees to capture morning light from the east. The bridge between them becomes a pause point: a moment of compression and transparency before the main volume opens up.

Materiality is deliberately restrained. Board-formed concrete anchors the home to its site, blackened steel frames create a precise graphic edge, and white oak wraps the interior volume in warmth. The Chief is the hero material — everything else defers to it.

Photography Approach

This shoot will orchestrate around the uninterrupted Chief sightline, with golden hour coverage of the west-facing elevation as the hero sequence — this is the awards-winning image. The two-bar plan geometry requires drone coverage to show how the offset volumes engage the topography, while the glass bridge becomes a critical interior moment: capturing both the compression-to-release experience and the external view of the gap between volumes.
