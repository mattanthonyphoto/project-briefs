Design Intent

A compact forest dwelling that disappears into the old-growth canopy of Crumpit Woods. The home is organized around a central double-height living volume with a glass gable end that frames a single ancient Douglas fir. Every design decision defers to the forest — the material palette is deliberately muted, letting the green light filtering through the canopy become the dominant interior finish.

The plan steps back from the trees rather than clearing them, resulting in an irregular footprint that wraps around three mature cedars. Covered walkways connect the main volume to a detached studio, creating a sense of moving through the forest to reach different parts of the home.
